First visit

Every first session follows the same arc. Below is what the hour looks like, minute by minute.

  1. 0:00 – 0:15

    Intake & assessment

    Range-of-motion screen, palpation, and a focused history.

  2. 0:15 – 0:55

    Treatment

    Sequenced manual therapy targeted at the findings — not a routine.

  3. 0:55 – 1:00

    Plan & protocol

    Reassessment, two or three at-home actions, and a return cadence.

What happens at a first session?+

About 15 minutes of intake and assessment, 40 minutes of treatment, and 5 minutes of plan and protocol. You'll leave with a working diagnosis, two or three at-home actions, and a recommended return cadence.

What should I wear?+

For any work over clothing — gua sha, stretching, or assessment — loose athletic wear is ideal. For oil-based work, you'll undress to your comfort level. Draping is professional and consistent throughout.

How early should I arrive?+

Five minutes is plenty. The intake form is online and arrives with your booking confirmation — fill it out at home, not in a waiting room.

Technique & expectation

Will it hurt?+

Trigger point work and some myofascial release can be temporarily intense — never sharp, never bruising. We talk through pressure as we go and adjust live. The goal is therapeutic, not heroic.

How is this different from a spa massage?+

A spa massage is built around relaxation. A clinical session is built around a finding. The work may feel similar in places — many of my clients do leave deeply relaxed — but the order, depth, and selection of technique are determined by what the assessment surfaces, not by a routine.

How often should I come?+

For an active issue, weekly for the first three or four sessions. For maintenance, every two to four weeks. For athletes in season, often weekly. We set the cadence together at the end of the first visit.

Will I be sore the next day?+

Sometimes — particularly after a deeper trigger-point or cupping session. It typically resolves in 24 to 48 hours. Hydration, light movement, and a warm shower are the standard at-home prescription.

Do you do deep tissue?+

I don't list "deep tissue" as a service because depth alone is not a treatment. I do work deeply when the tissue calls for it — that's what trigger point and sports technique often involve. If "deep" is your shorthand for "I have stubborn knots," yes, that's much of my practice.

Payment & insurance

Do you accept insurance?+

I do not bill insurance directly. I do accept HSA and FSA cards, and I provide a superbill on request that many clients submit for out-of-network reimbursement.

What's a superbill?+

An itemized receipt with the procedure (CPT) and diagnosis (ICD-10) codes your insurance needs. It does not guarantee reimbursement — that's between you and your plan — but it's what you'd attach to a claim.

Do packages expire?+

Packages are good for one full calendar year from purchase. They are transferable to a partner or family member if your circumstances change.

Perinatal

From which week of pregnancy can I book?+

Anytime in the second trimester onward without restriction. First-trimester sessions require an OB or midwife clearance. Postpartum work can begin as soon as you're cleared at your six-week check.

Are positions safe?+

Side-lying with full bolstering throughout. We adjust position multiple times per session.

Are there contraindications you screen for?+

Yes. Preeclampsia, gestational diabetes, placenta complications, history of preterm labor, and DVT history all require physician clearance. I screen at intake and won't proceed without it.
