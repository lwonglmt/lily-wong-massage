About Lily Wong

I am a New York State Licensed Massage Therapist and the founder of Lily Wong Massage Therapy PLLC, a private integrative massage therapy practice serving clients in Midtown Manhattan and Long Island.

My clinical background includes working in multidisciplinary settings alongside acupuncturists, physical therapists, and chiropractors — an environment that shaped how I approach every client: as a whole person with a specific presentation, not a service to be scheduled.

I hold a perinatal massage certification and am currently pursuing licensure in acupuncture at Pacific College of Health and Science, deepening the integrative foundation of this practice.

I am a proud member of the American Massage Therapy Association (AMTA), committed to the highest standards of professional practice and continuing education.

Methods used (as clinically appropriate)
Myofascial release
Sustained pressure to release restrictions in the connective tissue surrounding muscles.
Neuromuscular therapy
Targeted work on trigger points and nerve compression patterns contributing to pain and dysfunction.
Therapeutic & deep tissue work
Structured soft tissue manipulation addressing deeper layers of muscle and fascia.
Therapeutic stretching
Assisted range-of-motion and lengthening work integrated where clinically indicated.
Gua sha
Instrument-assisted soft tissue technique to address fascial restrictions, improve circulation, and reduce inflammation.
Cupping therapy
Negative pressure applied to soft tissue to decompress underlying structures and release chronic tension patterns.
Gua sha and cupping therapy may be incorporated into sessions where clinically indicated, at no additional charge.

Frequently asked questions.

Everything you need to know before your first visit.

Your first visit
Your first session begins with a detailed intake conversation. You will share information about your condition, medical history, and what you are hoping to address. This assessment guides the entire session — nothing is assumed. Once we have gone through your intake together, I will step out of the room to give you privacy to get changed. I will knock before re-entering and we will begin.
Wear whatever is comfortable — casual or athletic clothing works well. You are welcome to undress to whatever level you are comfortable with. The most important thing is that the areas being worked on are accessible.
Not at all. You undress only to your comfort level. Many clients choose to keep some or all of their clothing on, and sessions can be adapted accordingly. You will always be properly draped throughout your session.
Payments & insurance
Insurance is not accepted directly. However, a superbill is available upon request after each session. Many clients with PPO plans or out-of-network benefits are able to submit superbills for full or partial reimbursement — check with your provider.
Massage therapy may be eligible for FSA and HSA reimbursement, but it is not automatically covered — most plans require a Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN) from your physician or licensed medical doctor confirming that massage therapy is medically necessary to treat your specific condition. This letter must come from your doctor, not from me as your massage therapist. Once you have it, I can provide a superbill with the clinical codes your FSA or HSA administrator may need to process your reimbursement. I recommend checking with your plan administrator first to confirm their specific requirements.
A superbill is a detailed receipt that includes your session information, clinical procedure codes, and my license credentials. You can submit it to your insurance company for out-of-network reimbursement, or use it to document an FSA or HSA expense. Superbills are available upon request.

1. Who we are

Lily Wong Massage Therapy PLLC is a licensed massage therapy private practice located at 44 E 32nd St, 8th Floor, New York, NY 10016. Contact: lwonglmt@gmail.com.

2. Information we collect

We collect personal information through client intake forms, contact form submissions, session notes maintained after each visit, and testimonials submitted with your explicit consent.

3. How we use your information

Your information is used solely to provide and improve your care — to assess your condition, maintain clinical notes, communicate about scheduling and billing, coordinate care with referring providers (with your written consent), and display approved testimonials (with your written consent).

4. How we protect your information

Your health and personal information is handled with strict confidentiality. Client records are stored securely and are not shared with third parties without your written consent, except where required by law. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information.

5. Your health information and HIPAA

As a licensed healthcare practitioner, we handle your health information in accordance with applicable New York State laws and professional standards. Health information is used exclusively for providing care and will not be disclosed without your consent except as required by law.

6. Website and contact forms

Information submitted through the contact form is used solely to respond to your inquiry. We do not use analytics tools that collect personally identifiable information, and we do not place advertising cookies on this site.

7. Your rights

You have the right to request a copy of your personal information, request corrections, withdraw consent for testimonial use at any time, and request deletion subject to legal record-keeping requirements. Contact lwonglmt@gmail.com to exercise these rights.

8. Record retention

Client records are retained for a minimum of seven years following your last visit, in accordance with New York State professional licensing requirements. After this period, records are securely destroyed.
